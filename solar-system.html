SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System is a group of planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and other space objects that move around the Sun due to its gravitational force. The Sun is at the center of the Solar System and provides light and heat to all the planets. The Solar System was formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a giant cloud of gas and dust. There are eight major planets, and each planet has unique features, size, atmosphere, and temperature.

SUN

The Sun is a huge star made mainly of hydrogen and helium gases. It is the source of energy for the entire Solar System. Without the Sun, life on Earth would not exist.

IMPORTANT FEATURES

  • Largest object in the Solar System
  • Provides heat and light
  • Extremely hot
  • Strong gravitational force keeps planets in orbit

MECURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and also the smallest planet in the Solar System. It has a rocky surface filled with craters, similar to the Moon.


FEATURES

  • No atmosphere for protection
  • Very hot during the day and extremely cold at night
  • Takes 88 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun

VENUS

Venus is the hottest planet in the Solar System because of its thick atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide. It is often called Earth's twin because of its similar size.


FEATURES

  • Thick toxic atmosphere
  • Extremely high surface temperature
  • Rotates in the opposite direction compared to most planets

EARTH

Earth is the only known planet that supports life. It has water, oxygen, and suitable temperature conditions for living organisms.


FEATURES

  • 71% covered with water
  • Has one natural satellite called the Moon
  • Supports millions of life forms

MARS

Mars is known as the Red Planet because of the iron oxide (rust) on its surface. Scientists believe Mars may have had water in the past.


FEATURES

  • Thin atmosphere
  • Contains the largest volcano in the Solar System
  • Two moons: Phobos and Deimos

Neptune is the farthest known planet from the Sun. It is a cold and windy ice giant.
